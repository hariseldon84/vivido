## 🎯 Research Methodology

## 🔴 Descript — User Research

### What Users Love

- **Text-based editing is genuinely transformative.** Users describe deleting transcript lines and watching video auto-edit as "magic." The core concept works. "If you can edit text, you can edit video" lands in practice.
- **Studio Sound (AI audio cleanup)** is called out repeatedly as a standout — users recording in hotels, noisy rooms, or on bad mics rave about how close it sounds to a studio booth.
- **Overdub voice cloning** — users love fixing mistakes by typing a correction rather than re-recording. One reviewer called replacing "2024" with "2025" in their own cloned voice "creepy how legit undetectable it is."
- **Screen + webcam recording combo** — useful for tutorials, product demos, walkthroughs.
- **Filler word removal** (ums, ahs) saves enormous time for interview and podcast editors.
- **Team collaboration** — commenting directly on transcript timestamps called "way more efficient than emailing video files."
- Overall: users who invest time learning it become very loyal. "Switching to Descript was the best decision I've ever made for productivity gains."

### What Users Hate

- **Steep initial learning curve** — "too different from what people expect." Users who don't get past day 1 churn fast. The paradigm shift is too jarring without good onboarding.
- **Performance crashes on long files** — consistently reported for anything over 30–45 minutes. "I could not believe how unstable it had become."
- **Export quality is terrible.** A 500MB source file compressed to 23MB on export. YouTube rejects the quality. Multiple reviewers flag this as a dealbreaker. "This is why many people see Descript as a tool for the rough cut, not the final export."
- **Overdub is buggy** — voice cloning crashes on long scripts, voice runs too fast, bulk gap editing breaks frequently.
- **Support is slow and generic** — "takes several iterations before they understand the issue. They resort to video replies even for simple overdub bugs."
- **Removed Quick Recording (Loom-style feature)** without updating promotional materials — users feel deceived. Refused refunds to affected users.
- **Recent updates bloated the app** — long-time users feel new features added while core stability was ignored.
- **No real control over export settings** — you're stuck with whatever Descript decides to compress to.
- **Syncing/auto-save failures** — "We lost many hours of work. We paid our editor hourly and paid for the subscription for reliability. Both failed."

### What Users Want (Unmet Desires)

- Proper export quality controls — bitrate, codec choice, resolution targeting
- Faster performance on files > 20 minutes
- A reliable Loom-style quick recording that stayed in the product
- Better voice cloning stability — the concept is beloved, the execution is buggy
- More intuitive onboarding — "don't make me unlearn everything I know about editing"
- Transparent auto-save with version history they can trust

### 🟡 Vivido Opportunity from Descript

| Gap | Vivido Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Terrible export quality | Full export controls — bitrate, codec, resolution — as a first-class feature |
| Crashes on long files | Background processing architecture from day 1 |
| Onboarding friction | "First video in 3 minutes" onboarding flow — no paradigm shift required |
| Lost work / no versioning | Auto-save with 30-day version timeline, visible and transparent |
| Removed Loom-style recording | Vivido Quick Capture is core — never a deprecated feature |

## 🔴 [VEED.io](http://VEED.io) — User Research

### What Users Love

- **Zero-install browser access** — users consistently love "edit from any computer, anywhere." Strong appeal for freelancers, small teams, agencies.
- **Auto-subtitle generation** — "Previously it took us a day to add subtitles. Now under an hour." The subtitle engine is the product's most-loved feature across all review platforms.
- **Speed of simple edits** — drag-and-drop for quick clips, trims, and social-format exports is fast and frictionless.
- **Translation in 125 languages** — called a "game changer" for global content creators.
- **Team collaboration** — real-time editing with teammates praised by agency users.
- **Trustpilot + G2 rating: 4.6/5 from 932 reviews** — generally positive sentiment, especially from light users.
- Social media creators who need quick, polished clips for Instagram/YouTube Shorts say "it does everything I need."

### What Users Hate

- **Aggressive, misleading watermark on free plan** — the single most-mentioned complaint across Trustpilot. "The watermark is too big." Users feel baited.
- **Misleading pricing and billing** — users report double charges, inability to cancel, refund denials, referral campaigns that didn't pay out rewards. One agency described VEED as having "used people like me for free marketing."
- **Reverts finished work to previous versions without warning** — multiple Capterra reviewers report hours of lost work with no recovery option. "It was absolutely maddening."
- **Bugs and crashes** — "Crashes at least 5 times whenever I try to make a clip" (for 30–40 second clips). Browser lag on larger projects is consistently flagged.
- **AI features underperform** — eye contact correction changes eye color. Background removal is broken. "The AI parts of VEED are awful."
- **Customer support is useless** — "Their answer was always: we checked with the developer and there's nothing they can do." Multiple users escalated to bank disputes.
- **No integrations** — missing a feature that most competitors offer.
- **Projects can't be split into modules** — agency/course users have to create separate projects for every segment.
- **Timeline editing is too precise, requires manual timestamp entry** — no snap-to-grid for common edits.
- **No recording built in** for some users — "I wish you could record on VEED."

### What Users Want (Unmet Desires)

- Honest free plan — smaller watermark or time-limited rather than quality-crippled
- Work auto-saves with visible version history — losing work is the #1 rage-quit trigger
- Reliable AI features — eye contact correction that doesn't distort faces
- Project organisation — folders, modules, sub-projects within one workspace
- Recording built in — screen + camera inside the same tool
- Integrations (Google Drive, Notion, social schedulers)
- Real customer support with humans who can actually solve problems

### 🟡 Vivido Opportunity from VEED

| Gap | Vivido Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Lost work, no version history | 30-day visible version timeline — always |
| Billing trust issues | Transparent pricing, one-click cancel, no dark patterns |
| AI that breaks faces | AI enhancement only applied on user confirmation; previewed first |
| No recording inside tool | Unified session: record → edit → publish in one app |
| No project organisation | Full project OS — folders, series, brand kits, campaign grouping |
| Watermark aggression | Generous free tier; paid plans compete on value, not restriction |

## 🔴 [Riverside.fm](http://Riverside.fm) — User Research

### What Users Love

- **Local recording quality** — 4K video + uncompressed WAV audio recorded on each participant's device. "Being able to capture 4K regardless of a spotty internet connection is a total game-changer."
- **Separate track per participant** — each guest gets their own audio/video file. Essential for post-production flexibility.
- **Clean, fast setup** — guests join via browser link with no download. Hosts praise how easy it is to onboard guests.
- **AI summaries, show notes, and chapters** — auto-generated after recording. "The AI summaries are amazing."
- **Magic Clips** — AI extracts social-ready highlights from long recordings.
- **All-in-one appeal** — users who switched from Zoom or Meet feel they now get recording + editing + publishing in one place.
- Users save 10–15 hours per episode. "It's well worth the money."

### What Users Hate

- **Audio drift** — over time, audio no longer matches video. Lips don't align with words. Painful for professional podcasters who do final edit in external tools.
- **Editing tools are limited** — "I still edit all episodes in external tools because Riverside's editing can't do what I need." The editing layer is seen as a rough-cut tool, not a finisher.
- **Cannot animate graphics or text** — users compare this unfavourably to Canva.
- **Mobile app is underpowered** — lacks features available on desktop; not smooth enough for on-the-go editing.
- **Only Chrome/Edge supported** — no Firefox or Safari. macOS native app reportedly unstable.
- **Dark pattern cancellation** — Trustpilot review: "Signed up for free trial, charged $288 annually. The cancel button in their UI doesn't exist. Every exit point is deliberately broken." Filed with Australian Consumer Law authority.
- **Slow rendering on larger videos** — "Rendering time for bigger videos can be painfully slow when I'm on a tight deadline."
- **Shorts AI is weak** — one Capterra reviewer compared Riverside's Shorts AI unfavourably and switched tools specifically for it.
- **Customer support slow to resolve bugs** — multi-day waits, impersonal responses.

### What Users Want (Unmet Desires)

- Full editing power inside Riverside — not a rough-cut tool, a finisher
- Animated text/lower thirds and motion graphics inside the editor
- Reliable audio sync — the drift issue has persisted for years
- Better Shorts/clips AI — smarter hook detection, not just transcript-based extraction
- Mobile parity with desktop
- Trustworthy billing — visible cancel button, no dark patterns
- Background rendering — not blocking the workflow

### 🟡 Vivido Opportunity from Riverside

| Gap | Vivido Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Audio drift | Native sync engine; drift detection flagged automatically |
| Rough-cut only | Vivido is the finisher — export controls, colour, lower thirds, all inside |
| No animated graphics | Motion-capable lower thirds, title cards, animated captions as standard |
| Weak Shorts AI | AI clip engine built around hook-quality scoring, not just transcript |
| Billing dark patterns | Vivido's trust policy: cancel in 2 clicks, always |

## 🔴 CapCut — User Research

### What Users Love (Past tense — pre-controversy)

- Massively generous free plan — most features available without paying
- Mobile-first UX — tap-to-add effects, intuitive for Gen Z creators
- Beat sync — auto-cuts video to music rhythm
- Fast for 30–60 second clips
- TikTok-native effects and trending templates
- 1 billion+ downloads; deeply familiar to the global creator base

### What Users Hate (and Why They're Leaving)

- **US ban (Jan 2025)** — unavailable for new US downloads. Existing users have no future update or security patches.
- **Content ownership scandal** — June 2025 ToS grants ByteDance "perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide" rights to all uploaded content including private drafts. Agencies cannot legally use it for client work.
- **Price near-doubled** — Pro plan went from ~$8/mo to ~$15/mo in early 2026. Free plan gutted (dynamic captions, advanced effects moved behind paywall).
- **Weak for long-form** — no real multi-track timeline; falls apart at anything longer than 3–5 minutes.
- **No recording studio** — purely an edit/effects tool.
- **No collaboration** — single-user experience only.
- **No brand kit** — agencies cannot maintain consistent visual identity.

### What Former CapCut Users Need (Displacement Opportunity)

- A tool that's as intuitive as CapCut was — but trustworthy
- Generous free tier without content ownership traps
- Content data stays yours — guaranteed, in writing
- Short-form native (9:16, 1:1) but also capable of long-form
- Mobile-accessible, not mobile-only
- Brand kit for agencies

### 🟡 Vivido Opportunity from CapCut

| Gap | Vivido Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Data ownership nightmare | Vivido Pledge: your content is yours. Full stop. In ToS, in plain language |
| US ban / trust crisis | Vivido is indie, transparent, creator-owned philosophy |
| No long-form | Vivido does long-form as well as short-form — same workspace |
| No team collab | Agency/team seats with brand kits and role permissions |
| Free tier gutted | Vivido free tier generous by design — CapCut displacement play |

### The Dream Feature List — What Creators Actually Want

Based on verbatim requests across reviews:

1. **"I want to fix my mistake without re-recording"** → AI voice patch / overdub that actually works
2. **"I want my video done while I start the next one"** → Background rendering
3. **"I don't want to learn a new interface — just let me start"** → 3-minute first video onboarding
4. **"I want my brand to look consistent every time"** → Persistent brand kit applied automatically
5. **"I want one click from long video to Reels + blog + LinkedIn"** → Repurpose engine
6. **"I want to know which part of my video people will skip"** → Pre-upload retention scoring
7. **"I want to record my screen, my face, and a slide deck all at once"** → Unified session recording
8. **"I want captions that look like the big channels, not generic"** → Creator-style caption templates
9. **"I want to edit without touching the timeline at all"** → Full text-based editing that's stable and fast
10. **"I want to know my work is always saved"** → Auto-save with visible, trustworthy version history
11. **"I want the tool to know I'm a creator and give me a starting point"** → Creator Style Templates
12. **"I want my data to be mine, not some corporation's"** → Vivido Content Ownership Pledge
